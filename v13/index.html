LATEST RECOMMENDATIONS

A Selection of Distinguished Picks, Curated by Our Editors

MUSIC

Kind of Blue

Miles Davis · Album

Widely regarded as the finest achievement in the history of jazz recording, this 1959 masterwork remains an indispensable companion for those who seek beauty in restraint. Davis, accompanied by John Coltrane, Bill Evans, and an ensemble of unparalleled virtuosity, crafted an album that transcends the boundaries of genre and era. Its modal compositions unfold with the unhurried grace of a late afternoon, each note placed with the precision of a master calligrapher. One does not merely listen to this record — one inhabits it.

If you love Murakami, you will find in this album the same quiet melancholy and reverence for solitude that permeates his prose.

DINING

Ichiran Ramen

Hakata, Japan · Restaurant

In the narrow lanes of Hakata, where the steam of tonkotsu broth mingles with the evening air, Ichiran stands as a monument to singular devotion. Each guest is seated in a private booth — a confessional of sorts — where the only communion is between diner and bowl. The broth, simmered for eighteen hours, achieves a depth of flavour that eludes description. The noodles, firm and straight, carry the soup with quiet authority. It is dining reduced to its most essential and most exalted form.

If you love Kind of Blue, you will recognise the same philosophy of stripped-back perfection in every measured spoonful.

CINEMA

In the Mood for Love

Wong Kar-wai · Film

Wong Kar-wai's 2000 masterpiece is a study in the ache of proximity without possession. Set in the cramped corridors of 1960s Hong Kong, it follows two neighbours who discover their spouses are conducting an affair. What follows is not revenge but a restrained, devastating courtship of glances and near-misses. Cinematographer Christopher Doyle paints each frame in saturated reds and smoky hallways, while the recurring waltz of Shigeru Umebayashi's score marks the passage of time and longing in equal measure.

If you love Ichiran, you will appreciate this film's devotion to atmosphere, solitude, and the beauty of things left unsaid.
